# Wishees Charity / Organization Wish Safety And Growth Note - 2026-06-23

Context: side-chat question asked whether Wishees should create wishes for an organization such as UNICEF and add their payment details.

Decision: do not create wishes, campaigns, or payment destinations on behalf of famous organizations unless the organization or an authorized representative explicitly participates.

## Hard Rule

Never present Wishees as collecting, organizing, or routing money for UNICEF, a charity, school, orphanage, shelter, church, hospital, public figure, or creator unless that party has authorized the wishlist/campaign and owns or verifies the receiving payment account.

This means:

1. Do not use a famous charity name as if it is a Wishees partner without approval.
2. Do not add payment details for an organization unless the account is verified by the organization or authorized organizer.
3. Do not imply donations are tax-deductible, officially endorsed, or delivered to the organization unless verified.
4. Do not use vulnerable children, orphanages, poor people, or disaster language as emotional pressure.
5. Do not let a random user create a charity-branded wish that looks official.

## Safe Growth Version

The safe wedge is:

> Wishees helps approved organizers create a transparent gift wishlist for a real gift moment.

Examples:

- A parent creates a class gift wishlist.
- A teacher creates a classroom supplies wishlist.
- A local volunteer creates a community gift drive and clearly says who is organizing it.
- A creator creates a public wishlist for a cause they personally support, with clear ownership and no false charity endorsement.
- A nonprofit creates its own verified Wishees profile and payment destination.

## Product Direction

If Wishees wants this use case, build it as an `Organization / Cause Wishlist` flow:

1. Organizer identity: person or organization name.
2. Beneficiary disclosure: who receives the items or funds.
3. Payment ownership: who receives money, with verification before publishing as an organization.
4. Public label:
   - `Organizer-created wishlist` for normal users.
   - `Verified organization wishlist` only after verification.
5. Safety copy:
   - `This wishlist is organized by [name]. Wishees does not verify beneficiary claims unless marked verified.`
6. Report/flag button for misleading charity claims.

## Facebook Content Direction

Use community gift-drive content only in generic, safe language. Do not name UNICEF or any famous organization.

Good framing:

```text
Một cô giáo, một phụ huynh, hoặc một nhóm bạn có thể tạo wishlist cho một dịp chung: quà lớp học, đồ dùng cho bé, hoặc một buổi gây quỹ nhỏ.

Quan trọng nhất: người tổ chức phải rõ ràng, người nhận phải rõ ràng, và wishlist không nên giả như đến từ một tổ chức nếu chưa được xác nhận.
```

Avoid:

```text
Donate to UNICEF through Wishees.
Create wishes for orphan children and send money to them.
Support poor people through our wishlist.
```

## Recommended Facebook Post

Theme: `Community gift list`

Caption:

```text
Một wishlist không chỉ dùng cho sinh nhật.

Phụ huynh có thể gom quà lớp học. Cô giáo có thể chia sẻ đồ dùng cần thiết. Một nhóm bạn có thể tạo list cho một dịp chung.

Điều quan trọng: người tổ chức rõ ràng, mục đích rõ ràng, và một link dễ chia sẻ cho mọi người.

Tạo wishlist tại https://www.wishees.com/?lang=vi
```

Prompt:

```text
Create a high-quality realistic vertical image for Wishees social media. Format: 1080x1920, photorealistic, warm Vietnamese community gift moment. Scene: a clean classroom or community table with books, notebooks, small wrapped gifts, practical supplies, and handwritten gift tags. No charity logos, no famous organization names, no children in distress, no fake app UI. Add readable Vietnamese overlay text: "Một wishlist cho dịp chung". Add small subtle brand text: "Wishees.com". Warm, hopeful, practical, trustworthy, not sad, not manipulative.
```

## Growth Decision

This can become a strong growth angle, but only after the base Facebook page has recent normal gift content. Sequence:

1. Birthday / normal wishlist post.
2. Baby shower / family post.
3. Group gift post.
4. Community gift list post.
5. Only then test organizer/nonprofit outreach with verification language.

Do not make charity/organization the first visible growth angle. It is higher-trust, higher-risk, and needs clearer product safeguards.
