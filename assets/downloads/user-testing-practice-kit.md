# User Testing Practice Kit

Use this before practice tests, paid tests, or live interviews. The goal is not to sound polished. The goal is to be useful, observant, and honest.

## 10-minute setup

1. Close noisy apps and browser tabs.
2. Test microphone input and make sure the room is quiet.
3. Keep the task scenario visible.
4. Speak in short observations while you work.
5. Say what you expected before you click.
6. Say what surprised you after each screen changes.
7. If you are stuck, say what you looked for first.
8. Avoid personal data unless the test explicitly provides fake data.
9. Do not rush just to finish.
10. End with the biggest friction point and one improvement.

## Think-aloud phrases

- "I expected to find this under..."
- "This label makes me think..."
- "I am hesitating because..."
- "The next thing I would try is..."
- "This page gives me confidence because..."
- "This page feels risky because..."
- "If I were doing this for real, I would want..."

## Practice scenario

You are comparing two online tools for remote work. Find the pricing page, identify the cheapest paid plan, and decide whether the product seems trustworthy enough to try.

Record yourself for five minutes while you:

1. Open the homepage.
2. Explain what you think the product does.
3. Find pricing.
4. Identify the cheapest paid plan.
5. Name one trust signal.
6. Name one concern.
7. Give a final recommendation.

## Final summary template

"The main thing that worked well was [positive observation]. The biggest friction was [specific issue], because [reason]. I would improve it by [practical suggestion]."
